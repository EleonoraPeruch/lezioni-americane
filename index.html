A close reading of Italo Calvino’s Lezioni Americane: Leggerezza. 

Use of XML technologies to analyse the levels of interpretation in the text.

This project has been realized for the laboratory activies of the Master Degree in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge of the University of Bologna, under the supervision of prof. Francesca Tomasi. The aim of the project is to apply some XML technologies for the analysis of Italo Calvino's essay Lezioni americane, also know as Six Memos For The Next Millennium.

The text: Lezioni americane

What do these memos have to tell us?

Should have been six, we only have five; memos for the next millennium, our millennium; values of literature. Lezioni americane, or Six Memos For The Next Millennium, is the last text Calvino worked on in his life. A series of lectures written by our author in 1985 for the Charles Eliot Norton Lectures at Harvard, but unfortunately never delivered in that occasion.

Leggerezza, Rapidità, Esattezza, Visibilità, Molteplicità (Lightness, Quickness, Exactitude, Visibility, Multiplicity). What do these memos can tell us?

This project is meant to enrich a research on Calvino aiming at extracting in a formal way what is unsaid in the author’s text. “Unsaid” here means those elements that have an influence on the construction of and message transmitted by the author’s works, but which have mainly an implicit presence in the text.
